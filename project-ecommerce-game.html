Empirical Approach

Choosing the Right Method

To establish a causal relationship between playing the game and sales, we evaluated three potential empirical approaches. The goal: find a method that handles endogeneity and works with our limited data.

Selected
IV + 2SLS

Instrumental Variables with Two-Stage Least Squares. Isolates the effect of play on sales by using marketing team as an instrument.

Unsuitable
Multiple Imputation + IV

Alternating data loss pattern violates the "missing at random" assumption required for valid imputation.

Unsuitable
Difference-in-Difference

No pre/post treatment data available â€” only value purchased "last week" before game introduction.

Why IV + 2SLS?

Marketing team assignment strongly influences whether users play the game (first stage), allowing us to isolate the causal effect of play on value purchased (second stage). This approach handles endogeneity â€” the possibility that unobserved factors affect both playing and spending.

"The key problem for any regression analysis is endogeneity. IV + 2SLS isolates variables that could influence other independent variables, reducing bias."

Limitations

Important Caveats

While the results are statistically significant, there are critical limitations that warrant caution when interpreting these findings.

01
Small Sample Size

Only 81 complete rows out of 2,000 were available for analysis. Of these, just 14 were adults above 35.

02
Risk of Overfitting

R-squared of 98.15% is abnormally high, likely due to the small sample rather than true model accuracy.

03
Omitted Variables

User income, engagement time, and platform usage duration weren't collected but could significantly impact results.

Heteroscedasticity Present

The Breusch-Pagan test detected heteroscedasticity (p-value = 1.664eâ»Â¹Â²). Even with robust standard errors, this issue persists due to varying spending habits among users â€” an unobserved factor not in the dataset.
