Key Trends

What the Data Reveals

[IMAGE_03] Conversion by Discount Group

Trend 01

Higher Discounts = Higher Conversions

The data shows a clear pattern: 12.81% converted with no discount, 27.58% with 10%, and 35.71% with 20%.

The jump from 0% to 10% discount increased conversions by 14.77%. Going from 10% to 20% added another 8.13%.

Key Takeaway

Offering any discount dramatically outperforms no discount. But diminishing returns suggest the first discount tier provides the biggest impact.

Trend 02

Target Low-to-Medium Reservation Users

Most users make 1-8 reservations per month. These are the sweet spot for marketing â€” frequent enough to see value, but not yet loyal.

Low (1-3): First-time or infrequent users â€” offer attractive limited-time incentives
Moderate (4-6): Some experience â€” focus on retention incentives
High (7+): Regular users â€” consider loyalty & referral programs

Trend 03

Discounts Don't Increase Reservations

Surprisingly, discounts don't make users book more often. The median reservation count is 7 across all discount groups.

Discounts influence conversion decisions, not booking behavior. Users who don't reserve often won't suddenly become power users just because of discounts.

Important

Discounts are effective for conversion, but won't change user engagement patterns. Other strategies are needed to increase reservation frequency.
